Abstract

Recent advancements in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have enhanced reasoning but often rely on unstable external tools or explicit step-by-step text generation. Inspired by human cognition, we posit that thinking unfolds not linearly but through the dynamic interleaving of reasoning and perception within the mind.

We propose DMLR, a test-time dynamic multimodal latent reasoning framework. It employs confidence-guided latent policy gradient optimization to refine latent think tokens. Furthermore, a Dynamic Visual Injection Strategy (DVI) retrieves the most relevant visual features at each thought step.

Experiments across seven benchmarks show that DMLR significantly improves reasoning and perception performance while maintaining high inference efficiency.

Methodology

Latent Policy Optimization

Instead of generating explicit text, DMLR introduces learnable Latent Think Tokens ($ \mathcal{T} $). We use a confidence-guided reward function to optimize these tokens iteratively during test-time.

./images/method_latent.png

Dynamic Visual Injection

Humans don't stare at an entire image constantly. DVI mimics this by selecting only the most relevant Visual Patches based on the current thought state.
